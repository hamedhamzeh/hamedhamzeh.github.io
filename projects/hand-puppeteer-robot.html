Hand Puppeteer robot

The Hand Puppeteer Robot project was initially conceived as a tool for studying and assisting children with autism. The broader aim of the project evolved to explore the potential of remotely controlling robots through machine vision. The robot is a simple, yet innovative, representation of a hand with two degrees of freedom. It was designed with versatility in mind, making it adaptable for various applications beyond its original purpose. All components of the robot, including its body and arms, were created using 3D-printed materials and Plexiglas.

In this project, a NodeMCU devboard Utilized for wireless control via wifi. Additionally, an MPU6050 Gyro module was integrated to enable movement commands based on glove gestures, although this feature was later adapted due to changes in the project scope. In the final stage, one of my colleagues and I created a dataset specifically for detecting puppet poses and trained a YOLO v8 pose detection model to enhance the robot's functionality.

System Design

The robot consists of three arms, each equipped with a servo motor to control its movements. Another servo motor is dedicated to rotating the entire body, providing the robot with two degrees of freedom. The robot's mobility is further enhanced by a detachable two-motor, three-wheel mechanism, which allows it to move on surface when required. This modular design ensures that the robot can be easily adapted for different use cases, such as stationary manipulation or mobile interaction. The device is controlled via a NodeMCU board, which facilitates wireless communication through Wi-Fi, enabling remote operation.

Originally, the robot was intended to be controlled by a specially designed glove, with an MPU6050 Gyro module allowing for gesture-based commands. Although the glove project was discontinued, the robot's design was adjusted to incorporate machine vision-based control. Collaboratively, I and my colleague developed a YOLO v8 pose detection model to track puppet movements. This model was synchronized with the robot, allowing remote control based on hand gestures and enhancing the robot's interactive capabilities.
