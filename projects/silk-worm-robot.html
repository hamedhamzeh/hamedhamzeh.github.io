Silkworm robot

We are currently preparing a research paper for submission to a leading journal in the field of robotics. Our work presents a novel robotic design inspired by silkworms, aimed at enhancing capabilities for monitoring, surveillance, and inspection on various metal surfaces. Below is four samples of our experimnets and the abstract of our paper, outlining the key findings and contributions:

" In this paper we have introduced a novel robotic design inspired from silkworms. The proposed design allows the robot to move on different structures for monitoring, surveillance, and inspection. Similar to silkworms, the robot consists of several modules connected to each other using passive spring joints to allow silkworm type of flexibility in motion. Furthermore, each module has one degree of freedom at its spine allowing the module to match both concave and convex surfaces. The robot has been actually built and tested in real environments. The results validate its capability in moving on different surfaces. Furthermore, it has shown its robustness against partial instability in modules."
